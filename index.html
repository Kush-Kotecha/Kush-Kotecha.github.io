    Dissertation


    My dissertation topic was Wettability of aqueous LiTFSI on graphite using Molecular Dynamics in relation to energy storage systems.

    The initial report contains a discussion of different energy storage technologies from a sustainability perspective. The focus is placed on aqueous electrolytes - in particular LiTFSI - as a cheaper, safer, and more environmentally friendly alternative to current materials such as LiPF₆. Finally, there is a brief overview of how and why models are used.

    The dissertation report contains Molecular Dynamics theory (for both background and simulation) and informative instructions on input creation. There are 3 investigations which look at slightly different aspects of the system but all share the same goal of improving electrolyte performance.

    Hover over this to read the dissertation abstract This dissertation aims to understand the wetting properties of aqueous LiTFSI on an uncharged graphite electrode with respect to concentration, temperature, and surface type. Wetting is quantified by the contact angle between the droplet and electrode which is measured graphically using Python and qualitatively evaluated using Young’s Equation.

    A basic introduction to Molecular Dynamics is provided and the theory behind key components of the simulation have been discussed in detail. The procedure for input preparation is given along with all supplementary files required to completely reproduce simulation of a 6.00 molal droplet at 298K on a flat surface (appendix B).

    The results show that the contact angle of water droplets decreases significantly with the introduction of LiTFSI and then continues to decrease at a more subdued rate as further ions are added. Increasing temperature also causes the contact angle to decrease and evaporation of water further decreases the contact angle. At 75° and 100°C, and an initial concentration of 6.00 molal, perfect wetting is achieved. Contact angle results could not be drawn from the changes in surface however the simulations serve as a preliminary insight into intercalation of LiTFSI between graphite layers.

    The validity of these results is discussed and compared to existing experimental data with consideration for the limitations present within these simulations (notably the lack of potential difference and reactions). Relevant market analysis is conducted and ideas for the future of LiTFSI are explored. Further work includes identifying the individual effects of temperature, evaporation, and steam on the system, and a more detailed investigation of intercalation.

    PBR in Matlab

    For my 3ʳᵈ year design project, I modelled a packed bed reactor in MATLAB . Using a Mg-ZSM-5 catalyst, methanol and toluene preferentially react to form para-xylene over its meta- and ortho- isomers. The model calculates the flowrate as a function of catalyst mass or reactor length and also allows the user to perform sensitivity analysis on a number of variables including temperature, pressure, diameter, and feed ratios.

    Sustainable Utilities

    In the final year of my Chemical Engineering degree, the majority of my modules were based around supplying utilities, all of which I thoroughly enjoyed: Utility System Design, Nuclear Fuel Cycle, and Sustainable Energy Systems. I have attached some of my current energy system work which covers two main scenarios: improving existing systems so that they are more efficient and assessing available technologies for a new system.

    Analysis and Optimisation of Utility System Model

    The utility system model investigates an existing system which is improved by implementing additional units such as flash drums to produce steam for both heat and power to increase overall efficiency. Different configurations are evaluated and the final model is optimised.

    & District Heat and Power Centre Feasibility Study

    The district centre feasibility study compares sustainable technologies to supply heat and power demands. The main criteria include economics, environmental impacts, efficiencies, and thermal storage. Further considerations are given for the chosen technology such as social impacts and logistical issues.

    Both reports were conducted by the same group and contain my individual (in blue text) and team tasks.
